Welcome to NYC !

The New York City Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) is the department of the government of New York City that manages the city's water supply and works to reduce air, noise, and hazardous materials pollution. Despite significant improvements in recent years, air pollution in New York City is still a significant environmental threat. Improving air quality is a difficult task because there are many types of air pollutants that can come from millions of sources, inside and outside city boundaries.

Along with air quality initiatives by other City agencies, NYC-DEP is responsible for updating and enforcing the Air Pollution Control Code (Air Code) which has the goal to preserve, protect and improve the air resources of New York City.
